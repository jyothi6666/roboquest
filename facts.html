Good-to-know facts

What's the difference between a robot and a regular machine?

A machine like a toaster only does one fixed action. A robot senses its surroundings, makes a decision, and then acts — it can change its behavior based on what it notices, like a robot vacuum choosing a new path around a chair.

What is "AI" doing inside a robot?

AI is the decision-making software. Sensors gather information, and AI helps the robot figure out what that information means and what to do next — like recognizing a face or planning a route across a room.

Do I need to know math to build robots?

A little goes a long way. Angles help with turning, basic geometry helps with movement, and simple logic (if this, then that) is the heart of almost every robot program.

What's the easiest way to start at home or school?

Block-based coding platforms let you snap instructions together like puzzle pieces instead of typing code — it's the same starting point most professional roboticists used too.

What competitions can school students join?

Programs like FIRST Robotics and RoboCup Junior let student teams design, build, and program real robots to complete challenges against other schools.
