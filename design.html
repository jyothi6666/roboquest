What's a robot actually made of?

Every robot — from a tiny line-follower to a full-size humanoid — is built from the same five ingredients. Mix them differently and you get a totally different machine.

🦾

Chassis

The body and skeleton. It holds every other part together and decides the robot's shape — wheels, legs, or tracks.

👁️

Sensors

The robot's senses. Cameras, distance sensors, and microphones let it notice walls, light, sound, and objects around it.

🧠

Controller

The brain. A small computer (often a Raspberry Pi or microcontroller) reads the sensors and decides what to do next.

⚙️

Actuators

The muscles. Motors and servos turn electrical signals into real movement — spinning wheels, gripping claws, walking legs.

🔋

Power

The energy source. Batteries keep everything running — better batteries mean a robot can work longer without a recharge.

📐

Design loop

Real robot builders sketch, prototype, test, and redesign again and again — failure is just part of getting it right.
