How robotics is evolving right now

A quick, kid-friendly snapshot of where the field stands in 2026.

In classrooms

Robots are showing up as helpers, not replacements

Schools around the world are trying out classroom robots like NAO, Pepper, and the more affordable Alpha Mini to help teach coding and even practice languages. Every study agrees on one thing: robots work best alongside a real teacher, not instead of one.

Getting cheaper

Robots are becoming affordable enough for families

A few years ago, a walking, dancing humanoid robot cost as much as a car. New entry-level models now sell for close to the price of a good laptop, with simple drag-and-drop coding built in for beginners.

Smarter brains

Robots are starting to learn by watching

Instead of being programmed move-by-move, some new robots learn tasks by watching a person do them first, then practicing until they get it right — much like how you might learn a new skill by watching a video first.

Big moment

Humanoid robots took over the stage at CES 2026

This year's big tech show was packed with walking, talking humanoid robots from companies all over the world, showing off skills from folding laundry to running at a jog.

Why it matters for students

The fundamentals still come first

Educators keep repeating the same advice: before building a fancy humanoid, learn the basics — logical thinking, simple code, and hands-on building with beginner kits. Those skills carry over to any robot you'll ever work with.
